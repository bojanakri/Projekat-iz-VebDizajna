The first FEST, under the title "Brave New World", was opened on January 9, 1971 in the Dom of the Union, screening the film "Mes" by Robert Altman. Imagined as an annual cross-section of the best films of the season, the Belgrade International Film Festival FEST has grown into one of the most important film reviews and has become one of the main events in the cultural life of Belgrade, with whom many young generations grew. What has adorned it all the past years is the fact that it was cheerful for all cinematography of the world, and for all relevant authors, but also films that will not be found on the cinema repertoire. The importance of the FEST is reflected in the fact that is a Belgrade audience - with the films of some of the greatest directors of today, such as Teo Angelopulos, Bob Rafelson, Volker Schlendorf, David Kronenberg, Ken Russel, Vim Venders, Kischlovski, Hercog ... had the opportunity to meet for the first time on FEST. So far, FEST has had about four million viewers and nearly 4,000 significant world films - who had a Yugoslav / Serbian and often a regional premiere on it. Although the first FEST was held more than 40 years ago, this year with the same energy continues its big cultural and educational task to the horizons of the film horizon all lovers of film art.
